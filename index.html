My Japan Life So Far

My first month as a university student in Japan has been both exciting and challenging. Arriving in a new country was overwhelming at first—the language, the customs, even the convenience stores were completely different from what I was used to. But within days, I started to appreciate the rhythm of Japanese life.

University orientation helped me meet other international students, and I’ve already made friends from countries I never expected. Classes began smoothly, though getting used to the teaching style and navigating the campus took some time. I’ve also been practicing Japanese every day, and small victories—like ordering food in Japanese or asking for directions—feel incredibly rewarding.

On weekends, I’ve explored local temples, tried traditional dishes like okonomiyaki and ramen, and even joined a student club. While I do miss home sometimes, I’m grateful for this opportunity to grow. Living in Japan has already taught me so much about independence, communication, and cultural respect—and it's only been a month!
